5 Signs Hail Damaged Your Roof

Most homeowners don't realize their roof was compromised until a water stain spreads across the ceiling. By then, what could have been a simple repair has often doubled in cost.

Most insurance policies have time limits for reporting storm damage, which makes early detection especially important.

At Reyes Roofing & Remodeling, we've been inspecting Stillwater roofs for over 15 years. We drive the same streets you do—past the neighborhoods off Western Hills, near Boomer Lake, and through Couch Park. We know what these Oklahoma storms do to our community.

According to the National Weather Service, Oklahoma experiences some of the most frequent severe weather and hail events in the United States, making regular roof inspections an important part of home maintenance.

Here are five signs that hail may have damaged your roof—and why catching them early makes all the difference.

1. Missing or Cracked Shingles

Walk your property and look up (safely, from the ground). Look for bare spots, curled edges, or shingles split in half. If you see dark patches, your roof's underlayment is already exposed. Rain and sun will quickly rot the deck beneath.

2. Dented Gutters and Downspouts

Gutters take the first hit. Check your downspouts, window trim, and fascia boards. If they look like they went a few rounds with a baseball bat, your shingles took the same punishment—even if they look fine from the sidewalk.

3. Granules in the Downspouts

Run your hand inside your downspout or check the ground near the drain. Piles of black, sand-like grit are your shingles' protective granules washing away. Once they're gone, UV rays cook the matting underneath, accelerating your roof's aging by years.

4. Soft, Bruise-Like Marks on Shingles

A professional roof inspection may reveal soft spots beneath your shingles that feel similar to a bruise on a piece of fruit. This often means the hail has damaged the fiberglass mat underneath. Even if the roof looks fine from the ground today, these weakened areas can become leaks during the next heavy rain.

5. Interior Water Stains or Peeling Paint

Check your top-floor ceilings and attic walls. New yellow stains, bubbling paint, or peeling wallpaper mean moisture is already inside. Even if the roof looks perfect from the curb, this is a red flag that needs immediate attention.

Quick Hail Damage Checklist

■ Missing or cracked shingles
■ Dented gutters or fascia
■ Granules in downspouts
■ Spongy or soft shingles
■ Water stains inside your home

If you checked even one of these boxes, don't wait.

Addressing hail damage early is often far less expensive than waiting until leaks and structural problems develop.
